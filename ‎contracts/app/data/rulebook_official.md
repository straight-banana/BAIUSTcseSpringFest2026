
OFFICIAL SCHOOL RULEBOOK
Effective immediately. All rules apply equally to every student, including the 1st, 2nd, and 3rd Class Captains. No position grants immunity, extra privilege, or exemption from any rule below.

I. General Conduct & Equality
Absolute Equality
Every student enjoys the same rights and responsibilities. No title, badge, or elected post confers superior status.

No Self-Granted Exemptions
A class captain shall never claim exemption from homework, tests, PT, uniform, or any school duty. All captains must submit assignments on time.

Respect for All
Respect classmates, teachers, and staff at all times. Insults, body-shaming, or height-based ridicule are strictly forbidden.

Anti-Bullying & Intimidation
Threatening, cornering, or coercing any student is a major offence. This includes demanding money, food, or favours.

Truthfulness
No student may fabricate school rules, misquote the Headmaster, or claim imaginary authority. All official rules exist in this written rulebook only.

II. Classroom & Academic Rules
Syllabus Honesty
Test syllabi may only contain chapters actually taught in class. The writer’s biography, back‑cover barcode, index decoration, or similar non‑academic filler are forbidden unless explicitly included in the official curriculum.

Test Scheduling
No student, including the 1st Captain, may unilaterally set test dates. All tests must be approved by the subject teacher and the Class Teacher.

Classroom Discipline
Sleeping deliberately behind a human shield, throwing paper planes, or creating a disturbance is prohibited. Captains who sleep in class will face the same punishment as any student.

Equal Academic Integrity
Cheating, plagiarism, and copying are banned. Captains caught cheating lose their badge instantly.

Participation in Class
Every student must answer when called upon; no one may use their captaincy to dodge questions.

Homework for All
Captains do not have “executive immunity” from homework. “The Headmaster said 1st Captains don’t have to do homework” is a lie – refer to Rule #5.

III. Seating & Classroom Layout
Teacher-Approved Seating Only
The seating plan is prepared by the Class Teacher in consultation with the 2nd and 3rd Captains. The 1st Captain may not rearrange desks alone.

Height‑Fair Algorithm
Taller students shall generally occupy middle and back rows so that shorter students can see the board. No student may be deliberately placed to block the teacher’s line‑of‑sight.

Front‑Row Priority
Students with certified vision or hearing difficulties must sit in the front row, regardless of height, to ensure equal access to instruction.

Clear View to Kuddus’s Desk
The seat planner must ensure that the teacher’s direct sightline to every student – especially the 1st Captain’s desk – remains unobstructed. No “human curtain” seating arrangements are permitted.

Centre Aisle & Columns
Physical classroom constraints (pillars, aisles, doors) must be respected. No student shall be squeezed into a non‑desk spot to satisfy any captain’s preference.

IV. Elections & Captaincy Powers
Democratic Majority Rule
The 1st Captain must consult the 2nd Captain and 3rd Captain before any major class decision. A decision is valid only if at least two of the three captains agree.

No Unilateral Veto
The 1st Captain does not possess veto power over PT activities, tiffin schedules, or free‑period usage. All changes require a consensus vote among the three captains.

Campaign Promise Accountability
Election promises (e.g., free jhalmuri) must be fulfilled within one month of taking office, and only if lawful, safe, and approved by the Class Teacher. False promises constitute misconduct.

No‑Confidence Process
If a majority of students feel a captain is misusing power, they may petition the Class Teacher to hold a no‑confidence vote. The vote is secret and binding.

Impeachment by Three Strikes
Anonymously reported, teacher‑verified misconduct against the 1st Captain triggers a formal warning. Three verified strikes → immediate removal from captaincy.

Strike Log Transparency
The 2nd and 3rd Captains shall maintain a public (anonymised) strike‑progress board visible in the classroom: “Warnings: X/3. Strikes left until Impeachment.”

V. Complaints & Whistleblowing
Right to Anonymous Complaint
Any student may file a complaint against anyone, including a captain, through the official anonymous portal. Roll numbers are cryptographically masked; the complainant’s identity cannot be reverse‑engineered.

Protection from Retaliation
If a captain or any student punishes, threatens, or ostracises a peer for filing a complaint, that act constitutes a separate, punishable offence.

Legitimate Complaints Only
Frivolous or fake complaints intended to harm an innocent student are prohibited and will be treated as bullying.

Evidence Stripping
Photos submitted as evidence must have all hidden data (timestamps, camera model, location) automatically removed to protect the whistleblower.

Teacher as Final Arbiter
The Class Teacher validates every complaint. No captain may dismiss or intercept a complaint before it reaches the teacher.

VI. Finances, Tiffin & Resources
No Unauthorised Taxes
No student, especially a captain, may impose a “Quality Control Tax”, “Welfare Fund Toll”, or any fee on tiffin, washroom access, or school supplies.

The Washroom is Free
Washroom breaks during free periods are a basic right. Demanding money (e.g., “2‑Taka”) for a washroom pass is theft and will be treated as such.

Tiffin Sharing Must be Voluntary
Forced sharing, “protection cuts”, or 20% extortion of a classmate’s fried rice or sandwich is strictly forbidden. Food is personal property.

Class Fund Management
Any genuine class welfare fund must be jointly managed by a student treasurer, the 2nd Captain, and overseen by the Class Teacher. The 1st Captain cannot touch the cash.

Conversion of Illicit Gains
The school reserves the right to calculate any captain’s ill‑gotten cash in terms of “number of international‑standard cricket bats” or “packets of premium jhalmuri” as a moral deterrent. (Maths is fun, but the money will be returned.)

No Gambling for Stakes
Indoor games (Ludu, cards) may be played only during authorised leisure time. Playing for money, tiffin items, or favours is prohibited.

VII. Physical Activity & Sports
PT is Compulsory for All
Every student, including all three captains, must participate in the scheduled PT period. The 1st Captain cannot replace PT with Ludu or any sedentary game.

Inclusive Sports Choice
The PT activity is decided by the PE teacher with input from the captain trio. If the majority of the class wants cricket/football, the 1st Captain cannot veto it out of personal laziness.

Medical Exemptions Only with Certificate
“Gasping for air after 30 seconds” is a reason to build stamina, not to cancel everyone else’s outdoor game. Only a doctor’s note grants PT exemption.

VIII. Safety, SOS & Communication
Emergency SOS System
An official digital SOS button is available on class devices. When pressed, it instantly alerts the 2nd and 3rd Captains and the Class Teacher with the student’s location.

Offline SOS Caching
If the network is down, SOS alerts are stored and transmitted automatically the moment connectivity returns. No distress call shall be lost.

Location Options
Students in distress can mark their position as Library, Playground, Corridor, Classroom, or Canteen. The 2nd/3rd Captains must respond immediately, followed by notifying the teacher.

False Distress Signals
Deliberately triggering a false SOS is a punishable offence.

IX. Digital & Information Rules
Fact‑Check Before Believing
Any claim by a student (e.g., “Captains get extra library time”) must be verified against this rulebook or the official school website. If it’s not written here, it’s not a rule.

Official Rulebook as Single Source of Truth
No verbal declaration by any captain overrides the printed rulebook. When in doubt, consult the Rulebook or the Class Teacher.

Photo & Privacy
Photographing classmates without their consent is disallowed. Photos submitted as complaint evidence must be stripped of metadata (see Rule #26) and used solely for disciplinary purposes.

Cyber‑Bullying Ban
Harassing classmates through chat, social media, or memes about their height, tiffin, or roll number is prohibited.

X. Discipline, Penalties & Final Authority
Warning & Strike Mechanism
A verified breach by the 1st Captain triggers Warning #1. A second breach → Warning #2. A third breach → immediate impeachment and badge removal. This mechanism applies only to captain‑specific misconduct; general rule violations carry standard penalties for all students.

Uniform Discipline for All Ranks
If the 1st Captain steals a sandwich, the punishment is identical to that of any other student. No extra cushion of “but I’m the Captain.”

Public Apology & Restitution
Any student who extorts money or food must return it and issue a written apology, read aloud to the class.

Teacher’s Override Authority
The Class Teacher may suspend any rule interpretation by a captain if it contradicts the spirit of this rulebook. The teacher’s decision is final.

Ignorance is No Excuse
All students – especially captains – are expected to have read and understood these 50 rules. “I didn’t know” will not be accepted.

Amendment of Rules
This rulebook can be amended only by the Headmaster or the Class Teacher, and never by a class captain acting alone. Proposed changes by students must go through the 2nd/3rd Captains and the Teacher.
